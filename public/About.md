The language is called Crousian. It diverges from English not to break rules but to forgive them — recognizing that human expression has always been ahead of whatever grammar tries to contain it. If you understand, you understand.

The project at the center of Crousia is called Income Insurance. It is an attempt to answer a simple question: how do we ensure there is always enough for everyone? Not through charity. Not through redistribution as punishment. Through architecture — a system designed so that sufficiency is the floor, not the ceiling.

Income Insurance is not finished. It may never be finished in the way a product is finished. It is a living document, and that is precisely why you are here.

For a small contribution, you gain editing access to the master document. Not to read someone else's conclusions — to help reach them. The archive preserves every version so nothing is ever truly lost. What gets built here gets built by whoever cares enough to show up.

The King of Crousia is Jeremiah. He does not have all the answers. He has the questions, the infrastructure, and the willingness to work in public.

You are welcome here. Bring what you have.
